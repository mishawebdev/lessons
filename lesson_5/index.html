Summer Heat Embraces the World

In these warm summer days, the sky is clear and cloudless. The sun shines brightly, bringing warmth and joy. Thermometers rise to incredible levels, exceeding 30 degrees Celsius. It's a time when everyone dreams of escaping the heat, diving into the water, and enjoying the coolness.

Spring Rain Brings Renewal

Spring weather brings not only warmth but also rain. Raindrops create a musical symphony, rejuvenating nature. The green leaves of plants are fresh and juicy, and the smell of rain fills the air. The rains replenish water reservoirs and enrich the soil with valuable substances.

Autumn Wind and a Fairy Tale Palette of Colors

Autumn brings a sense of coziness and melancholy. Trees dress in bright attire: golden, red, orange, and brown hues. Leaves gently fall, dancing in the wind, and the autumn wind whispers gentle melodies. Such weather inspires memories and creativity.

Snowy Winter Tale: The Magic of the White Realm

The winter landscape covered in snow looks like a magical world. The white blanket creates an atmosphere of mystery and joyful mood. Snowflakes dance in the air, and icy patterns on the windows decorate the city. Winter is a time for winter fun, skiing, and gatherings with friends by a cozy fireplace.

Volvo FH, найбільш продавана вантажівка компанії, святкує 30-річний ювілей

Volvo FH - одна з найуспішніших моделей у галузі, продана в кількості близько 1,4 млн. од. на 80 ринках по всьому світу. Вона була випущена з повністю новим шасі, розробленим для забезпечення різних транспортних завдань. Силовий агрегат включав абсолютно новий 12-літровий двигун (16-літровий для Volvo FH16), а повністю нова кабіна забезпечувала хорошу аеродинаміку і високий комфорт для водія.

Серед інноваційних досягнень останніх трьох десятиліть - подушка безпеки водія, автоматизована коробка передач I-Shift і система Volvo Dynamic Steering, що забезпечує легкість керування і підвищену маневреність.

"Перше покоління Volvo FH змінило правила гри, повністю трансформувавши концепцію сучасної вантажівки. Відтоді ми продовжуємо встановлювати нові стандарти в галузі вантажних перевезень і випустили п'ять наступних поколінь цієї вантажівки. Наша формула успіху полягає у використанні найсучасніших технологій таким чином, щоб завжди додавати очевидні переваги для наших клієнтів і водіїв. Я дуже пишаюся тим, що Volvo FH - єдина вантажівка, яка тричі була обрана "Міжнародною вантажівкою року", як доказ наших успішних зусиль з безперервного вдосконалення та постійного розвитку", - заявляє Ілва Далерстедт, керівник сегменту далекобійних вантажівок Volvo Trucks.

Which European sites are being added to UNESCO's World Heritage List?

UNESCO has started to announce the new sites to be added to its World Heritage List. The UN-initiative list is a collection of landmarks of special significance to the human race. Whether through a remarkable human contribution or the sheer wonder of natural beauty, World Heritage Sites are protected to ensure they can be conserved for future generations to enjoy.

This month, the World Heritage Committee are meeting in Riyadh, Saudi Arabia, to inscribe a new set of sites into the list. The committee began with nominations not covered in 2022 before moving onto 2023’s nominations. Before the committee met, there were 1,157 World Heritage Sites. The list has already increased and has been announcing the new sites since 16 September.

Here are some of the most notable new sites announced across Europe so far.

Gordion – Türkiye

Located around 70km southwest of Ankara, this archaeological site is what remains of Gordion, the capital city of Phrygia. The ancient civilization was a contemporary of the Ancient Greeks with myths of their kings Gordias, Midas, and depicted in Homer’s ‘Iliad’.

Jewish-Medieval Heritage of Erfurt – Germany

Slap-bang in the middle of Germany, the city of Erfurt hosts an impressive array of preserved Middle Age Jewish cultural sites. Dating back from the 11th-14th century, the Old Synagogue, the Mikveh, and the Stone House paint a fascinating picture of Jewish life in Germany.

Modernist Kaunas: Architecture of Optimism, 1919-1939 – Lithuania

Between the First and Second World Wars, Latvia’s provincial town of Kaunas was entirely regenerated by the Modern Movement architecture philosophy. Still present today, Kaunas’ urban streets are a reminder of that fascinating era.

Old town of Kuldīga – Latvia

Another example of a walkable town whose streets fizzle with cultural heritage. Kuldīga grew from a tiny mediaeval hamlet in the 16th century into an important craft and trades city over 200 years. The changes to the architecture remain today.

Prehistoric Sites of Talayotic Menorca – Spain

Dating back as far as 1,600 BCE, communities have lived on Spain’s Menorca island. Structures, tools and burial places bring insight to the ways people lived on Menorca, as well as their rituals and habits over a millenia before Christianity came to European shores.

Viking-Age Ring Fortresses – Denmark

Across five different sites, there are monumental rings in Denmark demarking where Viking Fortresses once stood. Built between 970 and 980 CE, the Aggersborg, Fyrkat, Nonnebakken, Trelleborg and Borgring were all key locations in the Viking Empire’s Jelling Dynasty.

Žatec and the Landscape of Saaz Hops – Czechia

Go to Žatec and you’re guaranteed a good time if you’re a fan of beer. The fertile hop fields near the river Ohře provide the area with the base for its excellent beer industry that has provided industrial revolution to the mediaeval city.
